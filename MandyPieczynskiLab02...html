What was the Surrealism movement?

The Surrealism art form started in the late 1910s and 20s as a "literacy movement that experimented with a new mode of expression" This type of art is meant to challenge you and your conscious such as trying to understand what you are seeing and why you are seeing it maybe different from someone else. Pushing the boundaries of everyday art it is enhancing your imagination to make the impossible, possible.

Artists involved in this movement

  • Salvador Dali, this artist was known for one of his most famous pieces called "The Persistence of Memory"
  • Pablo Picasso, this aritst was known for one of his most famous surrealism pieces called "The Weeping Woman"
  • Rene Magritte, this artist was known for "The Son of Man"
